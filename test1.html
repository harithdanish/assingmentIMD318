The Mitsubishi Galant and Mitsubishi Eterna are closely related mid-size sedans that share many similarities in design, engineering, and performance. Both models were developed by Mitsubishi during the late 1980s and early 1990s, and they were built on the same platform, meaning they had identical chassis structures, dimensions, and suspension setups. This shared foundation made the two cars very similar in terms of overall driving dynamics, handling, and ride comfort.

In terms of exterior design, the Galant and Eterna featured nearly the same body shape and styling elements, such as the headlights, grille, and side profiles, with only minor cosmetic differences depending on the market or trim level. Inside the cabin, both cars had a similar dashboard layout, seating arrangement, and interior features, offering the same level of comfort and functionality for drivers and passengers alike.

Mechanically, the two models were offered with similar engine options, including 1.8-liter and 2.0-liter 4-cylinder engines, which provided reliable performance and decent fuel efficiency for their class. Because of these shared characteristics, the Mitsubishi Galant and Eterna delivered a very comparable driving experience, making them virtually interchangeable in many aspects, especially in markets where both were sold or rebadged.
